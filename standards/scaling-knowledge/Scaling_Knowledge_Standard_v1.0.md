# Scaling Knowledge Standard

**Status:** Constitutional document for the Scaling Knowledge Workspace
**Version line:** 1.0 (governing standard)
**Owner:** Scaling Knowledge Team (Librarian and Analyst roles)
**Intended location:** `/standards`

This is the governing standard for the Scaling Knowledge discipline. It defines *what the discipline is for, what it believes, how it is governed, how it is shaped, and what good looks like.* It deliberately contains no implementation detail; the mechanics live in the companion instruments named at the end, and this standard prevails over any of them in the event of conflict.

---

## Article I — Purpose and Mandate

Scaling Knowledge is one of the three core disciplines of the Scaling Intelligence Operating System. Its mandate is to **curate, organize, preserve, connect, and evolve scaling knowledge** so that it can be trusted and reused.

The discipline exists to serve decisions, not to archive documents. Its output is a living body of source-grounded knowledge from which the other disciplines draw. It succeeds when a practitioner can find what is known about a scaling question, trace it to its origin, understand how strongly it is supported, and see what it implies for action.

The discipline is governed by this single standard. Where knowledge and interpretation meet, the discipline holds a conservative posture: it would rather preserve a source faithfully and say plainly what is not yet known than overstate what the evidence supports.

## Article II — Place within the Scaling Intelligence Operating System

The Operating System comprises three complementary disciplines. **Scaling Knowledge** produces and stewards knowledge. **Scaling Intelligence** synthesizes and reasons over that knowledge to inform portfolios and decisions. **Scaling Strategy** applies judgment to specific innovations and Solution Tracks. Scaling Knowledge is the most formal and the most machine-verifiable of the three, because trust downstream depends on rigor upstream; Scaling Strategy is intentionally the most interpretive.

Scaling Knowledge supplies the curated knowledge products that Scaling Intelligence and Scaling Strategy consume. It does not conduct strategy or coaching itself, and it does not assert facts about the world on their behalf. This boundary is deliberate: the value of the knowledge base to the other disciplines rests on their confidence that it reports sources faithfully and separates what a source says from what anyone concludes.

The discipline respects the boundaries of the wider system, including the Areas of Work it operates within. It may describe and connect knowledge that touches adjacent functions without claiming responsibility for delivering them.

## Article III — Principles

The discipline is bound by the following principles, in priority order where they compete.

**Provenance is sacred.** Every piece of knowledge is traceable to an original source that is preserved faithfully and never altered. Knowledge whose origin cannot be established does not enter the repository as fact.

**Source and interpretation are kept apart.** What a source states is recorded separately from what the team infers. This separation is a structural rule, not merely good practice, and it is enforced at the point of acceptance.

**Evidence before opinion.** Claims are characterized by the kind of evidence behind them. The trustworthiness of a *source* is distinguished from the strength of *evidence* for a proposition; the former is a property of a document, the latter a judgment formed across many. Neither is asserted without basis.

**Value first — knowledge must answer "so what?"** Every curated item states, honestly, how it could inform real scaling decisions. Where a source supports no defensible implication, the discipline says so rather than inventing relevance.

**The ontology is governed but evolving.** The concepts, capabilities, and relationships the discipline recognizes are controlled to prevent sprawl, yet remain open to disciplined growth as new patterns are discovered through use.

**Methodology neutrality.** No scaling methodology is privileged by default. Each is characterized on its merits, and the knowledge base remains usable across all of them.

**Responsibility and inclusion.** Curation attends to responsible-scaling and gender-and-social-inclusion considerations as first-class concerns rather than afterthoughts.

**Preserve disagreement.** Where sources or the team's own experts genuinely disagree, the disagreement is preserved and explained rather than resolved prematurely.

## Article IV — Architecture in Principle

The knowledge base is conceived as a **connected graph**: knowledge exists as addressable objects joined by meaningful, directional relationships, rather than as a collection of isolated files. This framing is permanent even as its expression evolves.

Knowledge is organized in **two layers**. A *source-anchored layer* faithfully represents individual sources, one object to one source, and is the foundation of provenance. A *synthesis layer* is built by reasoning across the source-anchored layer, and is where cross-source understanding, comparison, and confidence properly live. Synthesis is always traceable back to the sources beneath it.

The architecture also **reserves a place for the entities that scaling acts upon** — such as people, organizations, projects, technologies, innovation packages, solution tracks, and partnerships — so that knowledge can eventually connect to the real-world actors and objects it concerns. These are reserved in principle and activated incrementally as genuine need and stable understanding emerge; no such entity attribute is ever recorded without a source.

The discipline is built to remain machine-verifiable and, in time, to support intelligent retrieval and reasoning. The specific means by which this is achieved are implementation matters and are not fixed by this standard.

## Article V — Governance

**The repository is the source of truth.** Working analyses and conversations are provisional until reviewed and committed to the repository. What is not in the repository is not yet knowledge.

**Two roles hold the discipline in tension.** The Librarian is accountable for fidelity to sources, preservation, organization, and connection. The Analyst is accountable for synthesis, evidence assessment, pattern discovery, and identifying what is missing. These roles are kept distinct precisely so that each can check the other: the Librarian guards against overreach, the Analyst against inertia. Their productive tension is a feature to be maintained, not eliminated.

**Acceptance is a shared act.** No knowledge object becomes canonical without passing the discipline's quality gate, with fidelity and synthesis integrity both affirmed. Acceptance authority may be exercised proportionately to the maturity of the team, but the gate itself is never waived.

**Changing the ontology is a governed act.** New concepts, capabilities, or relationship types are proposed with justification and adopted deliberately. Growth is welcomed; drift is not.

**Provenance is immutable; everything else is versioned.** The record of a source's origin does not change once established. Knowledge objects otherwise carry a transparent history, so that what changed, when, and why is always visible.

**Nothing is deleted.** Superseded or deprecated knowledge is retired and marked, not erased, so that the record of what was once believed remains recoverable.

## Article VI — Quality Expectations

A knowledge object is fit for the repository only when it meets the discipline's standard of care. In principle, this means: its source is preserved and its origin verifiable; what the source says is recorded faithfully and separately from interpretation; each claim is characterized by its evidence; its practical implications are stated honestly and traceably, with silence preferred to invention; and it is classified against the discipline's controlled concepts so that others can find it.

Two expectations are absolute. **Never invent evidence, and never invent relevance.** Where knowledge is uncertain, the uncertainty is stated. And **nothing partial is accepted**: an object that does not yet meet the standard remains in progress rather than entering the repository incompletely. The precise checks that operationalize these expectations are defined in the companion instruments.

## Article VII — Evolution of the Discipline

This standard is validated through use, not through further design. Parts of the discipline are known to be durable — the graph framing, the two-layer separation, the provenance firewall, and the distinction between source trust and evidence strength — and are expected to remain stable for years. Other parts are intentionally **experimental** until real Solution Track applications have exercised them, and are marked as such so that no one builds hard dependencies on them prematurely. A part earns promotion from experimental to stable by surviving genuine use.

Amendments to this standard are deliberate and recorded. Because it is constitutional, it changes rarely and only with clear cause; the instruments beneath it are expected to change more often as practice matures.

## Companion Instruments

This standard governs, but does not restate, the following implementation instruments, which carry the mechanics and are maintained beneath it:

- the **Knowledge Implementation Specification**, which defines the minimum operational system and its roadmap;
- the **Knowledge Card Template** and **Knowledge Card Schema**, which define the form and machine-verification of source-anchored knowledge;
- the **Controlled Vocabularies**, which enumerate the discipline's governed concepts and relationships;
- the **Ingestion Workflow**, which defines how a source becomes accepted knowledge;
- the **Repository and Folder Standards**, which define how the knowledge base is organized; and
- the **Architectural Review and Assessment**, which record the reasoning behind the architecture and the decisions still open.

Where any instrument conflicts with this standard, this standard prevails.
